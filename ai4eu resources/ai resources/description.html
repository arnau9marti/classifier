Main characteristic

DEMO: To showcase the solution, we have shared a brief video that shows some of the pothole detections and estimations: https://www.loom.com/share/3a11c1e7fa15475ca700668d202ce84c For any specific questions please feel to reach out. CHALLENGES: 1) Pothole detection (identifying that an image contains a pothole) This is handled via a tflite or pytorch model. We already have a computer vision model detecting this (see video demo link above) 2) Pothole dimension identification (length/width/radius mm, depth mm) At the moment, we estimate the length and width already (see video link). The depth is not estimated yet, but with sufficient high-resolution imagery, it will be feasible with a point cloud analysis or other depth models. 3) Rutting/Cracking detection (identifying that an image contains road rutting or cracking) This will be detected through a SOTA computer vision model (see video demo) 4) Pothole growth rate forecasting (how much a pothole is expected to grow over time) We will a module to determine the pothole growth through continuous data collection of the same potholes over a period of time. This will provide a data set from which regression models can be used. Based on the initial data exploration it might be of interest to include extra information based on specific road parameters if available (such as age, time-of-year) in terms of building a more robust prediction. 5) Pothole formation forecasting (identifying where potholes will form before they occur) A similar approach to growth rate, historical data will be used to track different road deteriorations and their transition into potholes. Decision trees could be used for such classification. NOTE The publication is made available to the open call team and evaluators

Detailed description

Hardware architecture: X64

The data processing of our current application is distributed between edge (android devices) and cloud computing. Further, the system takes advantage of the known camera parameters of the smartphone. Hence the current data pipeline is not immediately importable to a docker setup yet. During the project, the native android codebase will be converted into a dockerized setup and the model will be adapted to the dataset provided. This will ensure that the system runs seamlessly on the dockerized format specified for AI4EU.

GDPR requirements

People and license plates captured in the road environment are anonymized by the model. This is the same approach as other road-mapping solutions have used such as Google Maps, Mapillary, etc.

Trustworthy AI

With computer vision for road damage detection, municipality operators will be empowered with better and more transparent data regarding the road network than they previously had before. This is in part because the images from which the damages are detected are made available allowing for higher transparency than current inspection approaches. Having better road inspections also leads to more liveable and sustainable cities, as potholes not only can be dangerous for road users and damaging to cars but also accelerate the overall deterioration of the road if not fixed in due time. Lastly, as mentioned above, we ensure privacy by anonymizing people and license plates from the road environment.
